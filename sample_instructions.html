Project 3: Data Structures

Intro

With our new knowledge of data structures like lists and dictionaries, we’ve been hired by the local sporting goods store—Adventure City—to build a program that stores valuable inventory and employee information.

Let’s get started!

Step 1

Adventure City has the following items in their inventory:

  • 10 backpacks
  • 20 energybars
  • 7 sunglasses

In your text editor under the line that starts with “Step 1”:

Create a new dictionary called stock.
Add key-value pairs for backpacks, energybars, and sunglasses which map each item name (as a string) to the amount in stock (as integers).
Print stock.
Click "Test Code"
